<p><ul><li>N-linkage</li><li>O-linkage</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Glycoproteins are proteins that are modified by the addition of carbohydrate chains. There are different types of glycoproteins based on the linkage of carbohydrates to amino acids in the protein structure. N-linked glycoproteins have the carbohydrate chain attached to the nitrogen atom of asparagine residues. O-linked glycoproteins have the carbohydrate chain attached to the oxygen atom of serine or threonine residues. Other types of glycoproteins include C-linked and glycosylphosphatidylinositol (GPI)-anchored glycoproteins. Each type serves different functions in various biological processes.</p></p>
