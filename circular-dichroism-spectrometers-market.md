<p><p>Circular Dichroism Spectrometers are available in three types of light sources: Linearly Polarized Light Sources, Circularly Polarized Light Sources, and Multiple Light Sources. Linearly polarized light sources use light waves that oscillate in a single plane, while circularly polarized light sources have light waves that rotate in a circular motion. Multiple light sources combine both linear and circular polarizations for more accurate and detailed analysis. Each type offers different advantages and applications in studying the structural and functional properties of molecules.</p></p>
