<p><p>InGaAs cameras are available in two main market types: air-cooled and water-cooled. Air-cooled InGaAs cameras use air as a cooling mechanism to dissipate heat generated during operation. On the other hand, water-cooled InGaAs cameras utilize water for cooling purposes. Air-cooled cameras are suitable for applications where space and weight are limited, while water-cooled cameras are preferred for high-power applications where efficient heat dissipation is essential. Both types offer advantages depending on specific requirements and environmental conditions.</p></p>
