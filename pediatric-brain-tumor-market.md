<p><p>Pediatric brain tumors can be classified into different types based on their cell origin and characteristics. Gliomas are tumors that originate from glial cells in the brain, while Mixed Neuronal-Glial Tumors contain a mixture of both neural and glial cells. Embryonal Tumors are fast-growing tumors that occur in young children, typically in the cerebellum. Other types of pediatric brain tumors include Craniopharyngiomas, Choroid Plexus Tumors, and Ependymomas. Each type of tumor requires specific treatment approaches based on its unique characteristics.</p></p>
