<p><p>Wireless infrastructure refers to the physical components required for the transmission of wireless communication signals. Small cells are low-power wireless access points that can be deployed in areas with high data traffic to improve network capacity and coverage. Another trend is the adoption of cloud-based infrastructure solutions, which offer scalability and flexibility to network operators. Overall, the wireless infrastructure market is expected to continue growing as the demand for high-speed wireless connectivity continues to rise.</p></p>
