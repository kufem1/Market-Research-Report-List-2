<p><p>Through hole resistors are available in various types, two common ones being wirewound type and axial type. Wirewound resistors are made by winding a resistive wire around an insulating core, offering high precision and stability. They are commonly used in high-power applications. On the other hand, axial resistors have leads that come out of each end, making them easy to insert into a circuit board. They are widely used in electronic devices where space is limited, offering good resistance values and precision.</p></p>
